## Previewing Content

- Let's work through this step by step.

For investor and provider audiences, never over-emphasise — it reads as hype. Use sparingly:

- The critical point is...
- Don't overlook this...
- This is where the model earns its returns...
- The bottom line:...
- One thing that often gets missed...
- This is the part most investors get wrong.

## Providing Evidence

### Neutral attribution
- According to [Source]...
- Data from [Source] indicates...
- Research from [Organisation] found...
- The latest figures from DLUHC show...

### Supporting claims
- The structure supports this — here's why:...
- The numbers bear this out...
- This is consistent with what providers report...
- Case studies confirm...

### Qualifying language (use when data is directional not definitive)
- Typically...
- Based on current portfolio data...
- In most cases...
- Across the deals we've seen...

**Never:** Attribute claims to unnamed "experts" or "studies" without a real source.

## Question-Based Transitions

Useful for investor copy and LinkedIn posts. Must reflect real questions your audience actually asks:

- So how does this work in practice?
- What's the catch?
- How is this different from buy-to-let?
- Why haven't more investors heard of this?
- Is this compliant with current regulation?
- What happens if the provider can't pay?
- How long before you see returns?
- Still not clear on the structure? Here's the breakdown.

## Hedging Language — Compliance-Safe

Critical for property investment content. Always qualify projected returns:

- Typically...
- In most cases...
- Based on current market conditions...
- Subject to due diligence...
- Projected yield of [X]%, based on [specific deal type/location]
- Returns vary depending on lease structure and provider...
- This is indicative — actual figures depend on...

**Never use:** "guaranteed", "risk-free", "certain returns", "always"

---

## Transitions to Avoid — AI Tells

These phrases immediately undermine credibility with a sophisticated audience:

- "That being said,..." — use "That said,"
- "It's worth noting that..." — just say the thing
- "At its core,..." — never use
- "In today's fast-paced world,..." — never use
- "When it comes to the realm of..." — never use
- "This begs the question..." — incorrect usage, avoid
- "Let's delve into..." — never use
- "Navigating the complexities of..." — never use
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..." — never use
- "Game-changing" — banned, per citywide-voice
- "Revolutionary" — banned, per citywide-voice
- "Seamless" — banned, per citywide-voice

## Citywide-Specific Banned Phrases

These conflict directly with brand positioning or compliance requirements:

| Banned | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Guaranteed returns" | "Projected yield of X%, subject to lease terms" |
| "Passive income" (alone) | "Hands-off, lease-backed income model" |
| "Social housing" | "Supported housing" |
| "Tenant" (for providers) | "Housing provider" or "operator" |
| "Landlord" (in Housing Hub context) | "Property partner" or "investment partner" |
| "Interest" (return language) | "Net yield" or "projected return" |
| "Risk-free" | Never use — unqualifiable claim |
| "Get rich" | Never use |
| "Passive" without context | Explain the model — don't just claim it |
