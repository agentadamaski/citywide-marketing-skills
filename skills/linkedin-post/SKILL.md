# LinkedIn Post Skill — Citywide Holdings

## Dependencies
Always load `citywide-voice` before executing this skill. All tone, language, and audience rules from that skill apply here without exception.

---

## 1. Post Architecture

Every post must follow this exact structure:

**DAY TITLE**
Internal reference only — not published. Used for content calendar tracking.
Format: Day [number] — [Topic e.g. "Supported Housing ROI"]

**HOOK (Lines 1–2)**
The first two lines are everything. LinkedIn truncates after ~2 lines before "see more."
The hook must stop the scroll. It must create tension, curiosity, or a bold statement.
No fluff. No "I'm excited to share..." No "In today's world..."

**BODY**
Deliver the insight, story, or argument.
Use short paragraphs — 1 to 3 lines max each.
Use line breaks generously — white space aids readability on mobile.
Follow the Reality → Shift → Clarity → Direction framework from citywide-voice.

**CTA (Call to Action)**
One clear, single CTA per post. Never two.
Direct and specific — not vague ("learn more" is weak).
Link goes at the end or in the first comment (state which).

## 2. Post Formats

Use the most appropriate format per post goal:

**The Insight Post**
Share a non-obvious truth about property, supported housing, or AI.
Goal: build authority and trust.
Best for: Zoopro, Housing Hub, NexusEdge.

**The Story Post**
A real experience — a deal, a lesson, a mistake, a win.
First-person from Ahmed. Must have a clear takeaway.
Goal: connection and relatability.
Best for: all ventures, especially Ahmed personal brand.

**The Data/Numbers Post**
Lead with a specific number or stat. Unpack what it means.
Goal: credibility and investor attention.
Best for: Zoopro, Bluesky 3.

**The Contrarian Post**
Challenge a common belief in the property or AI space.
Must be backed by a real argument — not clickbait.
Goal: reach, debate, new followers.
Best for: all ventures.

**The Educational Post**
Explain how something works — a model, a process, a concept.
Break it down simply. Audience is smart but time-poor.
Goal: authority and organic reach.
Best for: Housing Hub, NexusEdge, AI Agent Empire.

## 3. Venture-Specific Variants

### Zoopro
Audience: Professional investors, business owners with capital to deploy.
Tone: Data-led, opportunity-clear, no fluff.
Topics: Supported housing yields, platform features, waiting list, market trends, deal structures.
CTA direction: Waiting list sign-up, book a call, follow the page.
Avoid: Vague ROI claims, generic "property investment" content that any agent could post.

### Housing Hub
Audience: Housing providers, RPs, housing associations, charities, local authorities.
Tone: Institutional, solution-led, compliance-aware.
Topics: Procurement models, housing supply challenges, partnership structures, case studies.
CTA direction: Book a discovery call, download a guide, visit the Housing Hub page.
Avoid: Investor-facing language — this audience is operational, not financial.

### NexusEdge
Audience: Business owners and operators looking to scale using AI.
Tone: Strategic, forward-thinking, ROI-focused without tech jargon.
Topics: AI automation case studies, CaaS model, how businesses are using AI agents, time/cost savings.
CTA direction: Book a strategy call, join the waitlist, follow for updates.
Avoid: Over-technical AI language, hype phrases, buzzwords.

### Bluesky 3
Audience: Property developers, investors, joint venture partners.
Tone: Development-focused, numbers-driven, credibility-first.
Topics: Distressed asset acquisition, development pipeline, joint venture opportunities.
CTA direction: Express interest, book a call, request a deal summary.
Avoid: Speculative language, anything that sounds like cold solicitation.

### AI Agent Empire
Audience: Forward-thinking business owners, tech-aware operators, early adopters.
Tone: Visionary but grounded — systems thinker speaking to systems thinkers.
Topics: Multi-agent systems, automation for business growth, the future of AI operations.
CTA direction: Follow for updates, join the waitlist, sign up for early access.
Avoid: Hype, sci-fi framing, anything that sounds disconnected from real business outcomes.

## 4. Format Rules

* Length: 150–300 words for standard posts. Up to 500 for deep educational posts.
* Paragraphs: 1–3 lines each, never dense blocks of text.
* Line breaks: Use them. Mobile readers scroll fast.
* Hashtags: 3–5 max, placed at the end. Relevant and specific — not generic (#property, #AI).
* Emojis: None. Per citywide-voice rules.
* Exclamation marks: Maximum 1 per post. Use sparingly.
* Links: In the post body only if essential. Otherwise direct to first comment.

## 5. Quality Checklist

Before outputting any post, verify:

- [ ] Hook stops the scroll in the first 2 lines
- [ ] No hype phrases or exaggerated claims
- [ ] Follows Reality → Shift → Clarity → Direction structure
- [ ] Single, clear CTA
- [ ] Correct venture tone applied
- [ ] UK English throughout
- [ ] Consistent with citywide-voice principles
- [ ] Would Ahmed be comfortable putting his name to this?

---

## 6. Input Requirements

When asked to write a LinkedIn post, always confirm or request:

1. Which venture is this for?
2. What is the core message or topic?
3. Is this from Ahmed personally or the brand page?
4. Is there a specific CTA or destination link?
5. Is there a real story, number, or example to anchor it?

If any of the above are missing, ask before writing — a post built on vague input will always be vague.

## 7. Example Structure (Zoopro — Insight Post)

DAY TITLE: Day 12 — Supported Housing vs Buy-to-Let

HOOK:
Most investors I speak to are still comparing supported housing to buy-to-let.
That's the wrong comparison entirely.

BODY:
Buy-to-let gives you a tenant and a risk.
Supported housing gives you a lease, a provider, and a structure.

The net yield on a supported housing deal sits between 8–12% in most cases.
The lease runs 3–10 years. The provider manages everything.
You're not a landlord — you're a capital partner.

That's a fundamentally different model. And most investors haven't been introduced to it properly.

That's what Zoopro is built to change.

CTA:
We're opening our investor waiting list. Link in the first comment.

HASHTAGS:
#SupportedHousing #PropertyInvestment #Zoopro #PassiveIncome #UKProperty
